Colorette started out in 2015 by \href{https://github.com/jorgebucaran}{\texttt{ @jorgebucaran}} as a lightweight alternative to \href{https://github.com/chalk/chalk}{\texttt{ Chalk}} and was introduced originally as \href{https://github.com/jorgebucaran/colorette/commit/b01b5b9961ceb7df878583a3002e836fae9e37ce}{\texttt{ Clor}}. Our terminal color detection logic borrows heavily from \href{https://github.com/sindresorhus}{\texttt{ @sindresorhus}} and \href{https://github.com/Qix-}{\texttt{ @\+Qix-\/}} work on Chalk. The idea of slicing strings to clear bleeding sequences was adapted from a similar technique used by \href{https://github.com/alexeyraspopov}{\texttt{ @alexeyraspopov}} in \href{https://github.com/alexeyraspopov/picocolors}{\texttt{ picocolors}}.
